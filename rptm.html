Ex-Machines

Ex-Machines acts as an archaeology of the anthropocene‚Äôs technological history and nostalgia. Parts of computers, white goods, broken electronics, housing from VCR‚Äôs. Our lives are inhabited by such machines which too have a life (and death in obsolescence) parallel to ours. A viewers nostalgia is explored through careful examination of a mix of aesthetics. The objects exude an enigmatic curiosity for classification and utility. Following documentation, the objects are dismantled and destroyed, only to exist in photographic form.

The redundancy of technology which is unfixable and has been discarded by its owners.

The amount of media technology discarded in cities is high and would be an easy source of material. These works involve photographic and scultural assemblages from re-using technological detritus.

These works create items which may look like new machine, or machine from the past which have been preserved. However, they have no function and are fictional in terms of existing for any purpose. I would aim to create 4 of these works of the period of the exhibition. This opportunity would allow for a new range of sculptural works to be produced from recycling the technological waste of the local area.
